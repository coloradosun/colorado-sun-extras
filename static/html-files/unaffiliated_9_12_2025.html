$17.7 millionHow much money the Colorado Department of Personnel and Administration is requesting for the program providing state employees with health, dental and life insurance.

The nearly $18 million ask was the largest supplemental budget request submitted by the Polis administration this week to the Joint Budget Committee.

The DPA says the money will be used to cover a funding deficit caused by the number of state employees switching to standard health insurance plans from high-deductible ones; an increase in high-dollar insurance claims; and a jump in the cost of covering GLP-1 treatment for state employees.

Glucagon-like peptide-1 medications like Ozempic and Wegovy are used to treat diabetes and obesity. The state decided earlier this year to stop covering new GLP-1 prescriptions for weight loss starting in July as part of a cost-cutting measure. State employees prescribed the drugs before July will continue to get coverage for the medications, albeit with a higher copay.

GLP-1 coverage for diabetes treatment was always, and will continue to be, exempt from the policy change.
